Fertilizer Guide for Vibrant Blooms

Roses need proper nutrition to grow healthy and bloom beautifully. Fertilizers play a key role in providing essential nutrients like nitrogen, phosphorus, and potassium. Nitrogen helps in producing lush green foliage, phosphorus promotes strong roots and abundant flowers, while potassium boosts overall plant strength and disease resistance. Organic fertilizers such as compost, cow manure, bone meal, or fish emulsion naturally enrich the soil. Chemical or slow-release fertilizers provide steady nourishment throughout the season. Regular feeding every 4–6 weeks during the active growing period ensures continuous flowering. Careful application avoids over-fertilizing, which can harm roots and reduce the quality of blooms.
